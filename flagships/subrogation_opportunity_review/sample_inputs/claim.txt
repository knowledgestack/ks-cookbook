LOSS NARRATIVE:

On March 15, 2026 at approximately 2:15 PM, the insured's dwelling sustained
significant water damage when a pressurized hot water supply line burst in the
upstairs bathroom. The supply line was part of a tankless water heater system
manufactured by AquaFlow Systems Inc. (Model TH-200X, installed September 2024
by licensed plumber Jim's Plumbing & Heating LLC).

The burst line released water for approximately 45 minutes before the insured
arrived home and shut off the main water valve. Water penetrated the second-floor
bathroom, hallway, and guest bedroom, and caused extensive damage to the
first-floor kitchen ceiling, cabinetry, and hardwood flooring directly below.

Preliminary damage assessment:
- Second floor: bathroom subfloor replacement, drywall replacement in hallway
  and guest bedroom, carpet replacement in guest bedroom. Estimated $38,000.
- First floor: kitchen ceiling replacement, cabinet refinishing/replacement,
  hardwood floor replacement in kitchen and dining area. Estimated $52,000.
- Personal property (Coverage C): damaged electronics, furniture, clothing.
  Estimated $12,000.
- Additional living expenses (Coverage D): family of 4 displaced for estimated
  3-4 weeks during repairs. Estimated $6,000.

Total estimated loss: $108,000.

THIRD-PARTY INFORMATION:

The insured reports that Jim's Plumbing & Heating LLC installed the AquaFlow
TH-200X unit and all supply lines in September 2024. The insured states the
plumber used flexible braided stainless steel supply lines rated for the
system's operating pressure.

An independent plumbing inspector (retained by the insured's own contractor)
examined the failed supply line on March 17, 2026 and noted that the crimp
fitting on the burst line showed signs of improper installation — the fitting
was not fully seated and showed tool marks inconsistent with the manufacturer's
installation specifications. The inspector's preliminary opinion is that the
failure was caused by improper crimping during installation, not a product
defect.

The AquaFlow TH-200X unit itself appears to be functioning normally and is not
believed to be the cause of the failure.

Jim's Plumbing & Heating LLC is a licensed contractor (LA License #PLB-44891)
and is believed to carry commercial general liability insurance.

ADJUSTER NOTES:

Coverage appears straightforward under HO-3 Coverage A (open-peril, water
damage from plumbing system is covered). No exclusions appear to apply — this
is not a flood, not gradual seepage, and not wear-and-tear. Claim assigned
to adjuster L. Martinez for field inspection scheduled March 20, 2026.

Reserve recommendation: $110,000 (moderate-to-severe tier per SOP CLM-2026-R2,
supervisor review required).
